  • Undergraduate Summer Research Assistant

    Trinity College Department of Physics (May 2025 – July 2025)

    Worked on the Physics Department at Trinity College on a high-speed photon coincidence detection system using an Alchitry Au Xilinx Artix-7 FPGA and a Python-based interface. As a team we managed to overclock the FPGA from its base-clock of 100 MHz to 300 MHz by using PLLs, critical path timing slack restructuring, and optimising the finite state machines. By overclocking the FPGA, we achieved an increase in the maximum rate of photon coincidence detection from 40 MHz to 110 MHz which counts for a x2.75 improvement in detection speed. Similarly, the minimum time window to detect a coincidence was decreased from 10 ns to 3.33 ns.
    This particularly aligns with my career endeavours, as I bridge software and hardware by developing low-level Verilog logic for real-time signal processing and sending it using UART for higher-level tools such as data visualisation and analysis. The application of this technology bridges the budget gap for physics laboratories for optics and photonics experiments, having an open-software and open-hardware approach where professors can access our platform online, purchase the required hardware, and just download the files to install and start using.
